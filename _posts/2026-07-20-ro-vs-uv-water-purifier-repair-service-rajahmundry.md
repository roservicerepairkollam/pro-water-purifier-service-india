<h2>RO vs UV Water Purifier – Which One for Your Home?</h2>

<p>The <strong>ro vs uv water purifier</strong> decision depends primarily on your water source and TDS level. Understanding the differences helps you choose the right system for your needs.</p>

<p><strong>How RO Works:</strong> RO (Reverse Osmosis) uses a semi-permeable membrane to remove dissolved salts, heavy metals, and chemicals. It removes up to 95% of Total Dissolved Solids (TDS), making it effective for high-TDS water. RO systems typically include UV disinfection to kill bacteria and viruses.</p>

<p><strong>How UV Works:</strong> UV (Ultraviolet) uses light to kill bacteria and viruses. However, UV cannot remove dissolved salts, heavy metals, or chemicals. It leaves TDS unchanged, so it doesn't improve taste or reduce hardness. UV is best for low-TDS municipal water that only requires bacterial disinfection.</p>

<p><strong>Which One for Godavari Water?</strong> For Godavari water with high TDS, RO is essential. The Godavari River and groundwater in East Godavari often have TDS levels exceeding 500 mg/L, with some areas recording over 2000 mg/L. A UV purifier alone cannot address the chemical contamination and high salt content. Only RO can effectively reduce TDS to safe levels. However, RO alone doesn't kill bacteria. That's why the best choice for Godavari water is an RO+UV combination system.</p>

<p><strong>Cost Comparison: RO vs UV</strong></p>

<p><strong>RO systems:</strong> Higher initial costs (₹8,000-16,000) but provide comprehensive purification. Higher maintenance costs with filter and membrane replacements. Produces wastewater (20-40%). Excellent water quality for all contaminants.</p>

<p><strong>UV systems:</strong> Lower initial costs (₹3,000-8,000) but cannot remove dissolved solids. Lower maintenance with only lamp replacement. Minimal water wastage. Good water quality for bacteria but poor for chemicals.</p>

<p><strong>The Verdict:</strong> For Godavari water, RO+UV is essential. UV alone cannot remove dissolved salts and heavy metals.</p>
