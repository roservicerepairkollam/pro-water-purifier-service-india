<h2>Why Commercial Buildings Need Dedicated RO Plants</h2>

<p>For commercial buildings, the water demand is substantial and constant. Domestic purifiers are inadequate for such volumes. <p><strong>Restaurants and food businesses:</strong> Purified water for cooking and serving.</p>

<p><strong>Key Components of a Commercial RO Plant:</strong></p>

<p><strong>Multi-media filter:</strong> Removes suspended particles like sand and silt, protecting downstream components.</p>

<p><strong>Activated carbon filter:</strong> Eliminates chlorine and organic compounds, preventing membrane damage.</p>

<p><strong>RO membrane:</strong> Core purification, removes up to 95% of TDS, reducing dissolved salts, heavy metals, and chemicals.</p>

<p><strong>Booster pump:</strong> Maintains necessary water pressure for efficient membrane operation.</p>

<p><strong>Storage tank:</strong> Stores treated water for distribution, guaranteeing ready supply during peak demand.</p>

<p><strong>UV disinfection:</strong> Final protection against pathogens, eliminating remaining bacteria and viruses.</p>
