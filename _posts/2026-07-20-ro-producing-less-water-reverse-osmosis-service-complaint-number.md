<h2>Why Is Your RO Producing Less Water?</h2>

<p>When your <strong>RO producing less water</strong>, it's typically due to one or more of the following issues:</p>

<p><strong>Clogged Pre-Filters:</strong> Sediment and particles accumulate over time, blocking water flow to the membrane. If your filters haven't been changed in 6-12 months, this is likely the cause.</p>

<p><strong>Fouled RO Membrane:</strong> Minerals and contaminants build up on the membrane surface, reducing its ability to pass water. High TDS water (common in East Godavari) accelerates this process.</p>

<p><strong>Low Water Pressure:</strong> Your RO system requires 40-60 psi to operate efficiently. If incoming pressure drops, water production slows significantly.</p>

<p><strong>Failing Booster Pump:</strong> The pump creates the pressure needed to push water through the membrane. A weak or failing pump means less water production.</p>

<p><strong>Air in the System:</strong> Trapped air can block water flow and cause unusual noises.</p>

<h3>Quick Troubleshooting Steps</h3>

<p>Before calling a professional, you can perform a few basic checks:</p>

<p><strong>Check the inlet valve:</strong> Ensure the water supply to your RO system is fully open.</p>

<p><strong>Check for leaks:</strong> Look for any visible leaks in the system.</p>

<p><strong>Check the storage tank:</strong> Is the tank empty? Sometimes the system is working but the tank isn't filling.</p>

<p><strong>Check the drain line:</strong> Ensure it isn't blocked or kinked.</p>

<p>If these steps don't resolve the issue, professional <strong>reverse osmosis service</strong> is required.</p>
