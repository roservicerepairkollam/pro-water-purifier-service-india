<h2>What Is a Booster Pump RO?</h2>

<p>A <strong>booster pump RO</strong> is a device that increases water pressure before it enters the RO membrane. It ensures the system has sufficient pressure to push water through the membrane, even when incoming water pressure is low. This is especially important in:</p>

<p><strong>Apartment buildings</strong> where water pressure is low.</p>
<p><strong>Areas with low municipal water pressure.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Borewell water sources</strong> with variable pressure.</p>
<p><strong>High-altitude locations.</strong></p>
<p><strong>East Godavari</strong> where water pressure can fluctuate.</p>

<p><strong>How Does a Booster Pump Work?</strong></p>

<p><strong>Water Intake:</strong> Water enters the pump from the supply line at low pressure.</p>
<p><strong>Pressure Increase:</strong> The pump increases water pressure to 60-100 psi output.</p>
<p><strong>RO Filtration:</strong> Pressurized water passes through the membrane for effective contaminant removal.</p>
<p><strong>Output:</strong> Purified water flows to the storage tank with adequate pressure maintained.</p>

<p><strong>Signs You Need a Booster Pump:</strong></p>

<p><strong>Low inlet pressure:</strong> Below 40 psi.</p>
<p><strong>Slow water production:</strong> Takes too long to fill the tank.</p>
<p><strong>High TDS in output:</strong> Membrane not working efficiently.</p>
<p><strong>System making noise:</strong> Pump struggling to work.</p>
<p><strong>Water wastage:</strong> More water going to drain.</p>
