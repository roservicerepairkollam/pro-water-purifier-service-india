<h2>How to Maintain Your Activated Carbon Filter</h2>

<p>The activated carbon filter removes chlorine, volatile organic compounds (VOCs), and other chemicals that affect taste and odor. Over time, these contaminants accumulate and the filter becomes exhausted. When this happens, chlorine and other chemicals can pass through into your drinking water.</p>

<p><strong>Signs Your Carbon Filter Needs Replacement:</strong></p>

<p><strong>Chlorine taste or smell:</strong> If your water tastes or smells like chlorine, your carbon filter is likely exhausted.</p>

<p><strong>Bad taste in general:</strong> Any unusual or unpleasant taste indicates the filter is no longer effective.</p>

<p><strong>RO red light blinking:</strong> Many modern systems alert you when filters need replacement.</p>

<p><strong>More than 6-12 months since last change:</strong> This is the standard replacement window for most carbon filters.</p>

<p><strong>Replacement Schedule:</strong> The pre-carbon filter should be replaced every 6-12 months. The post-carbon filter should be replaced every 9-12 months. In high-TDS water areas like East Godavari, you may need to replace them more frequently.</p>

<p><strong>Professional Replacement:</strong> While some homeowners change their own filters, a professional <strong>water purifier technician</strong> ensures correct installation, proper flushing, and system sanitization during replacement.</p>

<h2>How Often to Service RO Purifier</h2>

<p>Understanding <strong>how often to service ro purifier</strong> is essential for maintaining water quality and preventing costly breakdowns. The right schedule depends on water quality, usage, and system type.</p>

<p><strong>General Service Schedule:</strong></p>

<p><strong>Sediment filter:</strong> Every 6-12 months.</p>

<p><strong>Pre-carbon filter:</strong> Every 6-12 months.</p>

<p><strong>Post-carbon filter:</strong> Every 9-12 months.</p>

<p><strong>Complete system service:</strong> Every 6 months.</p>

<p><strong>System sanitization:</strong> Annually.</p>

<p><strong>RO membrane:</strong> Every 2-3 years.</p>

<p><strong>Factors Affecting Service Frequency:</strong></p>

<p><strong>High TDS water:</strong> Requires more frequent service, often every 3-4 months.</p>

<p><strong>Hard water:</strong> Causes faster filter and membrane wear.</p>

<p><strong>High usage:</strong> More water means faster component wear.</p>

<p><strong>Sediment in water:</strong> Causes pre-filters to clog faster.</p>

<p><strong>Signs Your RO Needs Service:</strong></p>

<p><strong>Reduced water flow:</strong> Indicates filters clogged or membrane fouled.</p>

<p><strong>Bad taste or odor:</strong> Means carbon filter is exhausted.</p>

<p><strong>RO red light blinking:</strong> Signals service is needed.</p>

<p><strong>Water leakage:</strong> Points to loose connections.</p>

<p><strong>High TDS output:</strong> Indicates membrane failing.</p>

<p><strong>System making noise:</strong> Indicates pump issues.</p>

<p>According to AO Smith, servicing your water purifier at least once every 6-12 months is recommended for optimal results.</p>
