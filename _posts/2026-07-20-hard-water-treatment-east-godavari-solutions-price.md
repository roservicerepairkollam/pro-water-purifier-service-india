<h2>Understanding the Hard Water Crisis in East Godavari</h2>

<p>Water hardness is caused by dissolved calcium and magnesium. In East Godavari, groundwater contamination from aquaculture and industrial effluents has made the problem worse. A study of the Godavari River basin showed TDS ranging from 40 to 550 mg/L, but local pollution spikes have pushed levels far beyond safe limits. This means the water you use for drinking and cooking is often much harder than the national average.</p>

<h3>Signs You Have Hard Water</h3>

<p><strong>White scaling on taps and fixtures:</strong> Mineral deposits from calcium and magnesium.</p>

<p><strong>Soap doesn't lather well:</strong> Hard water reacts with soap to form scum.</p>

<p><strong>Dry skin and dull hair:</strong> Minerals strip natural oils.</p>

<p><strong>Stained or spotted dishes:</strong> Mineral residue after washing.</p>

<p><strong>Low water pressure:</strong> Scale buildup in pipes.</p>

<p>Hard water can damage your appliances, shorten their life, and make them less efficient. It also affects your skin and hair—research suggests it can disrupt the skin barrier and increase the risk of eczema.</p>
