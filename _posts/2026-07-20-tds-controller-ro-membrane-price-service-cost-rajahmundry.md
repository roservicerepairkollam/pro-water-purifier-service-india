<h2>What Is a TDS Controller and How Does It Work?</h2>

<p>A <strong>TDS controller</strong> is a device that monitors and manages the Total Dissolved Solids level in your RO system. It works by measuring electrical conductivity and providing real-time feedback on water quality.</p>

<p><strong>What a TDS Controller Does:</strong></p>

<p><strong>Monitors TDS levels:</strong> Continuously measures dissolved solids in water.</p>
<p><strong>Controls the system:</strong> Triggers warnings when TDS is too high.</p>
<p><strong>Protects your health:</strong> Ensures contaminants are properly removed.</p>
<p><strong>Extends membrane life:</strong> Prevents damage from high TDS.</p>
<p><strong>Alerts you:</strong> Notifies when filters need replacement.</p>

<p><strong>How to Read Your TDS Controller:</strong></p>

<p><strong>Digital Display:</strong> Most modern systems show TDS in ppm (parts per million).</p>
<p><strong>Green Light:</strong> System working properly, TDS within safe range.</p>
<p><strong>Red Light or Blinking:</strong> TDS too high or service needed.</p>
<p><strong>Numbers Fluctuating:</strong> May indicate membrane issues.</p>

<p><strong>Ideal TDS Levels for RO Output:</strong></p>

<p><strong>Below 50 ppm:</strong> Excellent water quality (typical for RO systems).</p>
<p><strong>50-100 ppm:</strong> Good water quality, still acceptable.</p>
<p><strong>100-200 ppm:</strong> Fair water quality, may indicate membrane aging.</p>
<p><strong>Above 200 ppm:</strong> Poor water quality, membrane likely failing.</p>

<p><strong>What TDS Controller Doesn't Do:</strong></p>
<p>TDS controllers measure dissolved solids but cannot detect bacteria, viruses, or specific heavy metals. Regular professional testing is still essential for complete water safety.</p>
